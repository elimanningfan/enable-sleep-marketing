As a Dentist you're positioned to help treat sleep apnea.
But where do you start?

Your patients mention they snore loudly. Their partners say they stop breathing at night. They're exhausted despite "sleeping" eight hours. And when you examine them, you see the clinical signs—narrow airways, large tongues, crowded oral cavities.

You know oral appliance therapy could transform their health and quality of life.

But the path forward isn't clear:

  • • "What should I document at each visit?"
  • • "How do I connect with a sleep physician?"
  • • "What's my workflow from screening to treatment?"

The result? Many dentists avoid sleep medicine entirely, and millions of patients who could be helped remain undiagnosed.
